H2Orca was a fun collaborative project among five creators. My primary function was director and lead of the project- overseeing the varying departments, designing mockups of the finished design, and boosting team moral. This project was displayed in Normal Illinois' Children Discovery Museum and was designed to create future environmentalists by educating children on the impacts of oil spills in the ocean. We created a tank for them where they can use our versions of real up-and-coming technology that would help eliminate oil spills in the ocean. It was one of three exhibits temporarily displayed and it was by far the most successful. We had adults and children return to experience H2Orca again, and again.

During my time at Illinois State University, I had the pleasure of taking a kinetic sculpture class where we combined moters, pulleys, and other technology with wood, metal, and other forms of sculpture work. This particular installation was my final project for the class and one I am very proud of. We were tasked with creating moving sculpture that was affected by light. I love triangles and circles and always inspired by nature. Through trial and error and a lot of exploration, I created this revovling mountan piece. The different shadows playing off each other from different light sources plus one strobing light creates a whimsical and classic piece.

I had the opportunity to get a glimpse into game design using Unity while studying Arts Technology at Illinois State University. The class set us up with the ability to make simple games where you can collect objects in order to complete a goal. As a huge lover of the paranormal, I used my knowledge of Blender and Unity to design a simple horror-esque game. The player is a member of the Paranormal Hunters Society and has the opportunity to investigate a haunted house. You have to find and capture all the ghosts in the house before you lose your nerve and flee the house in fear.

To play, simply use the arrow keys to move around. In order to capture the ghosts, walk right into them! Be careful of playful spirits that will make noises and break items just to scare you!

Shelter Dash is one of my first games designed using Processing. It's a Frogger style gameplay- avoid the objects flowing horizontally across the screen to meet your end goal. Tetris Draw is my very first introduction to Processing and Javascript. The concept was a drawing application but to mimic the digital environment it lives in and was created by. Most drawing applications that are coded and my fellow classmates coded, were smooth and designed to mimic Microsoft Paint, or natural drawing outside of the computer. I wanted to dive deeper into the cyber world with a drawing application. So like any 8-bit game, or like Tetris with different colored blocks stacking on top of one another, I designed a drawing application inspired by those two themes. Directions are located right next to the drawing interface to ensure easy and fun drawing.
