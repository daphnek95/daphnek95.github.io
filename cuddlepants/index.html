The Cuddlepants was a collaborative project with a dear friend of mine, Cassidy Fisher. It started off as an assignment in an expanded media course at Illinois State University but turned into a creative exploration. Our task was to design and code wearable technology. What started off as a silly idea became reality when we created wearable pants with a pillow that would stroke the head of the person who rested their head in your lap.

At the time, Cassidy and I had just discovered professional cuddlers and it made us think of how there is a lack of physical touch or social connection in our digital age. As a sort of fun commentary on this, we sewed a pillow onto a pair of sweatshorts as well as rig an acrylic arm to the pants. We placed a button sensor into the pillow so when the button is pressed, the acrylic arm starts a swining motion to stroke someone's head. The idea is that the wearer's hands are free to browse the internet on their phone or play games without having to give up an arm to cuddle their friend or significant other.
