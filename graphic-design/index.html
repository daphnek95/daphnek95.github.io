This design was created in Procreate on an iPad Pro and then saved as a PNG and dropped into Cricut software, where it is cut out on a Cricut Joy and applied to resin projects that are sold on my artist shop. After being scaled and cut on the Cricut Joy, a tumbler is decorated and sealed with resin and once cured the vinyl is applied and sealed with Mod Podge before more resin is added on top. This particular design is inspired by Starbucks and my favorite game series, Diablo. It was originally designed to bring in the Halloween season.

These spooktacular coasters are inspired by one of my favorite attractions at Walt Disney World- The Haunted Mansion. I drew inspiration from the game series Diablo and The Haunted Mansion to bring to life four different skeletal coasters for Halloween. These coasters were created by first cutting out a circle on stamp material, pouring a silicone mold over the top, and then pouring resin in small layers- adding vinyl and flowers in between different pours. The vinyl designs were created on Procreate on an iPad Pro, then dropped into Cricut design software and cut on a Cricut Joy. These coasters are currently for sale on my artist shop.

While at Illinois State University, one of my favorite classes was 3D Design. In that class we focused on 3D printing and sculpting using Blender. One of our first projects we printed in that class were coffee cup sleeves. Our only requirement was to experiment with the shape and design of the traditional coffee cup sleeve- we had to think and design in a way that affects the way the cup feels and sits in someone's hand, to make it a unique experience. My design was inspired by bronze cufflets often worn by warriors and beginner level players in many RPG games. In particular I was inspired by an old online game, Runescape. The pointed out design is not sharp enough to stab or scrape the user, but offers the perfect indentation for your fingers to rest on, between or around. On one section of the sleeve there is a slight curve inwards for the thumb to rest perfectly. It is hefty and pleasing to hold, while still maintaining a fun unique design.

While studying 3D design, my teacher challenged the class to recreate a common household item but to change one aspect of it that would render the object useless. It didn't take long for the concept of a balloon lightbulb to pop into my head. How would the balloon stay full? Would you have to replace a bulb when too much air escaped? What shape would the balloon take? Realistically a balloon would not be a sustainable replacement to glass, especially when you add in the electricity of a lightbulb. The balloon would most likely pop. This was a fun exploration at product design with an exciting twist.

This particular design is an example of how handbills would be saved and prepared to be printed. The hunger banquet was a special event to raise awareness to the realities of poverty and world hunger. What started out as a weekly task my sophomore year of college, turned into a new passion of mine. My very first class for Arts Technology tried to instil daily artistic habits in its students to help nourish and build the artist within. We individually decided what habit we would build and reported every week the progress we've made on our habit. I've always loved enamel pins- I have a huge collection of Disney pins tucked away safely in my home- so it seemed only natural to explore the process of pin making and to sketch out a new pin design every day. Now, some sketches were better than others, but I am a huge fan of the parannormal and all things Halloween/spooky, and this particular design was my favorite I had made that semester. For the next year I saved up money and ended up producing 100 of these pins and brought them to life. It was an extrodinary process to collaborate with other artists to bring my fleshed out design into a physical enamel pin. I still have some for sale in my artist shop, and they are quite a favorite among my customers. This eventually led me to resin work and moldmaking where I make my own resin pins.
