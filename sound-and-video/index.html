She Kills Monsters Promo

Illinois State University's School of Theater performed a piece by Qui Nguyen called She Kills Monsters in October, 2017. This particular play deals with some serious issues and concepts that high schoolers deal with and shows them how to overcome it, so the School of Theater wanted to create trailers for the play to get high schoolers and the surrounding community to see the particular play. The trailer I created focuses on the whimsy of high schoolers doodling in their notebooks.

The Impossible Trail

Working in Blender, I created an animation for Greyscalegorilla's five second animation project. The category I chose to work with was Video Games. I remember how much I loved playing the Oregon Trail on my computer growing up. I also remember how bad I was at it and how frustratingly easy it was to die in the game. So I ran with the challenge of the game to parody it.

Speech Underscore

While at Illinois State University I took a sound design course. In it, we discussed how much music influences the tone and emotion of a speech. Therefore we created two different audio files using the same speech but with two different soundtracks underneath to display the differences in the speech's meaning.

Song one: Past the Edge by Kevin MacLeod Song two: Blizzard by Kai Engel

Mini Documentary - Breaking Barriers

Breaking Barriers is a miniature documentary created for a Web Video course at Illinois State University. Mental health and illness is a very fascinating topic for me and I wanted to create a video that immerses viewers into the minds of a sufferer of OCD and Depression. While people talk about their feelings and experiences with mental illness, visually the viewers are experiencing different compulsions.

Songs: Subdivision of the Masses and Even When We Fall by Philipp Weigl

Sound Effects Narrative

While taking a sound design course at ISU, we were tasked to create a narrative using only sound effects. We were not allowed to include any dialogue what-so-ever. With my particular piece, I was inspired by the 1984 film Red Dawn. America has been invaded by a new Nazi regime and soldiers constantly march along the once quiet neighborhood streets. This day to day life has become normal for our main character, until one day all hell breaks lose.
