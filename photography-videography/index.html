During my last semester at Illinois State University, I was given the opportunity to produce and film, alongside three other students, a promotional video for the Arts Techology program, now known as Creative Technologies. My role was primarily director, as we went with my presented concept. Not only did I direct and film the varying scenes, I also edited the "wizarding world" portion of our promo. The premise of the promo was as follows: as arts technology students we have the creative minds and learn the skills to create the fantasy worlds around us. A tired ATK (Arts Technology Student) falls asleep while working on a project and is guided by a golden snitch into different creative worlds- the wizarding world of Harry Potter, to a galaxy far far away with Star Wars, and eventually down a sewer pipe through Mario's crazy world. Every team member was responsible for editing a particular segment of the video before being carefully placed together.

When I first started learning Blender, 3D design, and 3D animation I learned about an artist challenge called the '5 second Challenge' by creator GreyScaleGorilla. The artist posts roughly three new catagories every month for artists to choose and are then challenged to animate a five second animation based on a word or theme. When I partook in the challenge, video games was one of the themes and was immediately drawn to it. I had several ideas going back to classic games I would play as a child- Putt Putt, Freddie Fish, Frogger, etc. It didn't take long before I remembered a game I had a truly love-hate relationship with- The Oregon Trail. This game was HARD. I never won. Most people I knew who played that game almost always died as well. So the concept of The Impossible Trail was made! Before this poor family even embarks onto the trail, they quickly die a very sad death.

View the trail for yourself here!

While studying animation and video editing at Illinois State University I was given the opportunity to put together a promotional video for the university's theater department where they would be performing a play called She Kills Monsters. Before putting anything together on Adobe After Effects, I met with the leading ladies of the show, held individual photo shoots with them, and filmed a live-action roleplay group on campus. Since the play takes on a similar style to live-action roleplay (or LARPing) and often flashes between that and the modern world, capturing footage of LARPing in action seemed crucial to the aesthetic of the promotional video. After some photo editing in Photoshop, everything was brought over to After Effects to bring the video together.
